HISTORY IN THE MAKING

Bradford Street

In 1767, Henry Bradford donated free land here to anyone willing to establish a trade. It’s been suggested that this was the catalyst for Digbeth’s growth along industrial lines. At one point, there were over 20 pubs on Bradford Street catering for workers. Today, there are just three.

Fazeley Street

The street is in the centre of Birmingham’s former industrial heartland and was part of the Italian quarter up until the Second World War. A symbol of Digbeth’s renaissance is Fazeley Studios, a key creative venue housed in a converted 150-year-old church. The studios are home for tech businesses specialising in disciplines ranging from holographics to virtual reality. They also house the Fazeley Social Restaurant and Bar as well as a café and meeting rooms for events and corporate functions.

Digbeth High Street

The street once made for a picturesque thoroughfare, far removed from its later industrialised look. Historian William Dargue recounts how there were once many timber-framed buildings, “which had been fronted with handsome brick facades in Georgian times”. Industrial plants such as Phoenix Works, Birmingham Battery and Metal Works and Devonshire Works, the former Alfred Bird & Sons plant, could be found on the high street. Sir Alfred’s former plant, founded in 1837, is now home to the Custard Factory, a centre for creative businesses and projects that officially opened in August 1993. Today, the high street is home to venues including The Irish Centre, centrepoint of a community that has been in the city since the 1600s, and the O2 Institute nightclub. A St Patrick’s Day Parade has passed along the road since 1952 and is now the world’s largest after New York and Dublin. The JFK memorial is one of the brightest and most recognisable murals in Digbeth.

Heath Mill Lane

The lane once provided a route from the main road to a corn mill founded by the de Birmingham family in the 13th Century. It’s a key part of Digbeth in an area where the creative industries have taken shape in former industrial buildings. Rhubarb, which offers office space to creative businesses, is part of the regeneration – the humorous name being a reference to the former Custard Factory

IGNITING CREATIVITY

Clean Kilo

The UK’s largest zero-waste supermarket - Clean Kilo follows an ethos of sourcing local and selling plastic-free. Described as pioneers in food retail, The Clean Kilo has heavily contributed to the resurgence of Digbeth High Street and continues to work with both Birmingham City Council and the local community to spread their message - educating young people on plastic pollution and developing initiatives to tackle food waste.

Digbeth Dining Club

A street food market that launched in 2012, Digbeth Dining Club is one of the quarter’s biggest draws. Open every Thursday, Friday and Saturday, DDC is a showcase of the best food vendors in the Midlands, nestled amongst the backstreets and archways that make up Digbeth. Combining award-winning street food and vibrant events, all backed by soundtracks from homegrown artists and music collectives, Digbeth Dining Club is a demonstration of everything that makes Digbeth great.

Baked in Brick

A Digbeth Dining Club alumni helmed by an award-winning street food vendor, Baked in Brick is one of Digbeth’s most exciting new restaurants. Featuring a full-size Mini Cooper in the wall (a throwback to when owner Lee Desanges would cook street food under the bonnet of a 1964 model Mini) and a mouth-watering menu of wood-fired pizza or chicken tikka wraps, Baked in Brick is a self-made success story that started life at the heart of Digbeth.

The Framers

Offering a bespoke picture framing service for clients, galleries and corporations - The Framers is a perfect example of the artistic talent that can be found in and around Digbeth. One of the first businesses to be established within The Custard Factory, The Framers also houses the Studio 4 gallery.

Custard Factory

Possibly the most iconic building in Digbeth, the Custard Factory represents the creative heart and soul of the quarter. Officially described as an independent shopping destination and creative digital workspace, The Custard Factory hosts everything from events, movies, cafes and shopping to offices, creative areas and communal spaces. Originally built in 1906 to produce Bird’s Custard, the Custard Factory remains an icon amongst the growing Birmingham landscape.

Irish community

The annual St Patrick’s Day parade and festival is the focal point of the Irish community – turning parts of the city into a sea of green. The Emerald Isle celebration is the third biggest in the world, attracting around 90,000 people a year and beaten only by New York City and then Dublin. The major community event traditionally includes a parade, music stage, street food stalls and Emerald Mile fun run. Birmingham is said to have the largest Irish population in the UK as well as Britain's only Irish Quarter, centred on Digbeth. The parade goes back to 1952 and was the first of its kind in the UK. It's taken place in Digbeth every year since 1996.

Birmingham’s Golden Age of Development

Birmingham Rising

Birmingham is a global destination experiencing a golden age of development, brimming with the confidence afforded by huge inward investment, incredible amenities and a thriving cultural scene. As cranes fill the skyline, the second city is completely transforming its landscape and infrastructure, building a smart, refined commercial offering while delivering extraordinary residential and leisure spaces. Rich in independent culture, retail, leisure and gastronomy, Birmingham consistently outranks other major UK cities for quality of life, affordability and career options. With the arrival of HS2 in 2026, direct access to the capital will make Birmingham an even more attractive choice for London commuters seeking the richness and vibrancy that Birmingham has in abundance.

Fueling the Fires of Business

The youngest city in Europe, Birmingham and its surroundings are home to over one million jobs located amongst global brands such as HSBC, PwC, the BBC and the Royal Bank of Scotland. By developing a talented workforce across a number of progressive sectors, Birmingham has built the largest business, professional and financial services hub outside of London. This commercial centre of excellence employs more than 100,000 people, attracting the eyes of the world and a variety of world-class occupiers.

Big City Plan

Birmingham’s Golden Age of Development was kickstarted by the Big City Plan - a 20-year project to revitalise and transform Birmingham’s key quarters. Covering every aspect of the ‘built environment’, the Big City Plan features a huge development pipeline including the Bullring, the regeneration of New Street Station, Grand Central, HS2, Birmingham Smithfield, Paradise, Arena Central and the Midland Metro Expansion, revitalising commercial, residential and infrastructure developments in the second city. It includes aspirations to create 50,000 new jobs and contribute £2.1billion to the economy each year and deliver exciting new public and commercial spaces. Focused on regenerating key areas around Birmingham, the ‘Big City Plan’ is one of the most ambitious, far-reaching development projects undertaken in the UK.

HS2 (Curzon Street Station)

HS2 will revolutionise connectivity and travel between Birmingham and London, reducing commuting times between the two cities to just 49 minutes and boosting Birmingham’s own economy by 4.2%. HS2 is designed to be the backbone of the rail network and would eventually connect 8 of Britain’s largest cities and over 30 million people, creating 30,000 jobs and delivering an estimated £40 million for the community.

Midland Metro Expansion
(part of Eastside regeneration)

The Midland Metro will help boost connectivity across the wider West Midlands, offering direct links to Edgbaston, the NEC and Wolverhampton as well as connecting a rejuvenated Eastside district and One Connaught Square to the rest of Birmingham. The expansion is estimated to be completed by 2022 and forecasting a GDV of £137 million.

Birmingham Smithfield
the Core of the City

Birmingham Smithfield has a unique history as the ‘birthplace’ of Birmingham, where the second city started life as a market town before evolving into the sophisticated global hub it is today. The Smithfield Masterplan will reimagine and revitalise Birmingham’s Eastside and Southside quarters, injecting £1.5 billion into the creative district and transforming the region into an exciting destination to live, work and play. Delivering 2,000 houses. Over 20 years, Birmingham Smithfield will add £470 million GVA to the local economy and create nearly 3,000 new jobs.

Arena Central

A major development in the heart of the city, Arena Central has a GDV of £500 million and will span 1.2 million sq.ft of work, retail and leisure space alongside 670,000 sq.ft of grade-A office accommodation. A huge development for the city centre, Arena Central will also be the new home of HSBC, HMRC and University of Birmingham facilities.

Paradise

With an estimated GDV of £700 million, Paradise will comprise 120,000 sq.ft of retail and leisure space, three new public squares and grade-A commercial space. A new destination at the heart of the city, Paradise will also be the new home of PwC’s regional headquarters.
